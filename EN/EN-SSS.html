Frequently Asked Questions

FAQ

How does Botox work?

Botox works by affecting the mimic muscles, inhibiting their function. Thanks to the application area and its effectiveness within a 1-2 cm radius, the lack of muscle activity prevents the formation of wrinkles in the skin.

Is there an age limit for Botox?

There is no specific age limit for Botox application; however, it is generally applicable from the age of 18 onwards. The decision should be based on the individual's skin condition and needs.

When does Botox take effect?

The effects of Botox typically become noticeable within 3-7 days after application, with full effects observed within two weeks.

Are there risks associated with Botox application? Can it be applied to everyone?

There are certain risks associated with Botox application, including headaches, temporary muscle weakness, and bruising at the injection site. It may not be suitable for everyone; it is generally not recommended for pregnant or breastfeeding individuals and those with certain neurological conditions.

What precautions should be taken after Botox?

Some important precautions after Botox application include:

  • Avoid lying down immediately after the procedure.
  • Refrain from rubbing or massaging the injection area.
  • Avoid intense exercise for the first 24 hours.
How often should Botox be repeated?

Botox applications are typically repeated every 3-6 months. However, this interval may vary based on individual skin type, age, and the effectiveness of the treatment.

Which areas are treated with Botox for sweating, and how is it done? How long does the effect last?

Botox for sweating is usually applied to areas such as the armpits, palms, and soles of the feet. The application involves injecting small doses into the affected areas. The effects generally last between 6-12 months.

How long does nose filler last?

The longevity of nose filler is typically between 12-15 months. After this period, the longevity of the filler can increase with subsequent applications.

In which situations should Botox not be applied?

Botox should not be applied during pregnancy, breastfeeding, or in individuals with certain neurological disorders, bleeding disorders, or a history of botulism. It should also not be administered to infected or inflamed skin areas.

Does Botox completely eliminate wrinkles?

Botox can significantly reduce the appearance of wrinkles, but it does not completely eliminate them. Additional treatment methods may be necessary for deeper wrinkles.

Will I have an expressionless, dull face if I get Botox?

When applied correctly, Botox does not create an expressionless face. The application should maintain your facial expressions while providing a natural appearance. However, excessive dosage can lead to a lack of expression.

What does FDA approved mean?

FDA approved indicates that a product (in this case, Botox) has been deemed safe and effective by the United States Food and Drug Administration. This approval signifies that the product meets specific standards.

Will my face look worse when the effects of Botox wear off?

Once the effects of Botox wear off, the face returns to its previous state. This means that the appearance prior to the Botox application will return, and it does not negatively impact skin health.

What is aesthetic filler?

Aesthetic filler refers to substances used to smooth out wrinkles in the skin and to fill areas where volume is desired.

Are there any risks associated with fillers?

Fillers are generally considered safe; however, like any medical procedure, they can carry risks such as allergic reactions, infection, or uneven results.

Is there a limit to the amount of filler that can be used? Can it be obvious that filler has been applied?

There is typically a recommended limit to the amount of filler that can be safely injected in one session, depending on the area being treated. If applied correctly, fillers should not be obvious; however, excessive use can lead to an unnatural appearance.

How long do fillers last?

The longevity of fillers varies depending on the type used, but they generally last from 6 months to 2 years.

How is filler applied? Is there pain during the procedure?

Fillers are usually injected into the desired area using a fine needle. Some patients may experience mild discomfort during the procedure, but many fillers contain a local anesthetic to minimize pain.

Is the result of the filler immediately visible?

Yes, the results of filler application are typically visible immediately after the procedure, although there may be some swelling that can affect the final appearance for a few days.

What is the difference between the Russian lip technique and the natural lip technique?

The Russian lip technique focuses on creating a more voluminous and defined lip shape, often with a more pronounced cupid's bow, while the natural lip technique aims for a subtle enhancement that maintains the lips' natural shape.

How many milliliters of filler are sufficient for lip augmentation?

The amount of filler needed for lip augmentation can vary based on individual preferences and desired outcomes, but typically, 0.5 to 1 ml is sufficient for a noticeable enhancement.

How long does nose filler last?

Nose fillers generally last between 12 to 18 months, depending on the type of filler used and individual factors such as metabolism and lifestyle.

What points should be considered when deciding to get fillers?

First and foremost, the qualifications of the administering physician and the quality of the product should be scrutinized. It is essential to look for FDA approval and to review previous applications done with the same filler.

What should you pay attention to after filler application?

After filler application, it is important to avoid strenuous activities, excessive sun exposure, and touching or massaging the treated area for at least 24 hours. Following the post-procedure care instructions provided by your physician is crucial.

How do filler substances work?

Filler substances work by adding volume to specific areas of the skin, thereby smoothing out wrinkles and enhancing facial contours. They can also stimulate collagen production in some cases.

Can fillers cause side effects?

Yes, fillers can cause side effects, which may include swelling, bruising, redness, and in rare cases, allergic reactions or infection. Most side effects are temporary and resolve within a few days.

Where can fillers be applied?
  • Fillers can be applied to various areas, including the lips, cheeks, nasolabial folds, jawline, and under the eyes. 
What is the active ingredient in under-eye light fillers? How do they differ from fillers used on the face?

The active ingredient in under-eye light fillers is often hyaluronic acid, which is specifically formulated to be lighter and less viscous than fillers used on other areas of the face. This allows for a more natural appearance and reduces the risk of complications in the delicate under-eye area.

Is under-eye light filler a permanent treatment? Does it have any side effects?

Under-eye light filler is not a permanent treatment; its effects typically last between 6 to 12 months. Potential side effects can include swelling, bruising, and in rare cases, more serious complications.

Is there an age limit for under-eye light fillers?

There is generally no strict age limit for under-eye light fillers, but it is typically recommended for individuals over the age of 18. The decision should be based on individual needs and skin condition.

Can some fillers used on the face be injected under the eyes?

While some fillers designed for facial use can technically be injected under the eyes, it is not recommended due to the different properties required for the delicate under-eye area. It is best to use fillers specifically formulated for that region.

What is jawline filler? What is its purpose? How long does it last?

Jawline filler is a type of dermal filler used to enhance the definition and contour of the jawline. It is typically used to create a more sculpted appearance and can last between 12 to 18 months, depending on the type of filler used and individual factors.
